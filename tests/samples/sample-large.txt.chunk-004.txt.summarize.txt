Sri Lanka has a free education system established in 1945, providing universal free education from primary to tertiary stage. It has 17 public universities, but faces challenges such as a lack of responsiveness to labour market requirements and disparities in access to quality education. The country has a rich cultural tradition influenced by Buddhism and Hinduism, with a variety of traditional dances and music. Sri Lanka's media landscape includes 192 newspapers, 25 TV stations and 58 radio stations. However, freedom of the press has been criticized as being among the poorest in democratic countries. The country has been criticized for human rights violations by several groups, including Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch. The culture is heavily influenced by Buddhism and Hinduism, and the national sport is volleyball, although cricket is the most popular.
