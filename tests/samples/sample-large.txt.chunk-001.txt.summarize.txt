The text provides a detailed history of Sri Lanka from the 13th century to the present day. It covers the shifting capitals, invasions, and the influence of various ethnic groups and foreign powers. The Portuguese arrived in 1505, followed by the Dutch and then the British, who took control in 1815. The country gained independence in 1948 and became a republic named Sri Lanka in 1972. The late 20th century was marked by civil war and ethnic tensions, particularly between the Sinhalese and Tamil populations. The war ended in 2009 with the defeat of the Tamil Tigers. Recent history includes the 2019 Easter bombings and a severe economic crisis. The text also describes Sri Lanka's geography, including its rivers, waterfalls, and coastline.
