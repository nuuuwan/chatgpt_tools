Sri Lanka, officially known as the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, is an island country in South Asia with a population of approximately 22 million people. The country has a rich history dating back 3,000 years and was a major trading hub due to its strategic location. It was colonised by the British from 1815 to 1948, and gained independence on 4 February 1948. Sri Lanka is a developing nation with the second-highest per capita income in South Asia. The country is rich in biodiversity and has a democratic republic political system. Sri Lanka's economy was initially a plantation economy known for cinnamon, rubber, and tea production, but now the main economic sectors are tourism, tea export, clothing, rice production, and other agricultural products. The country has a free education system and a rich cultural tradition influenced by Buddhism and Hinduism. However, it has been criticized for human rights violations and poor freedom of the press. Recently, Sri Lanka has been facing a severe economic crisis, leading to the resignation of President Gotabaya Rajapaksa.
